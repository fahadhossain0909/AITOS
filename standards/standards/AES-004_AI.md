# AES-004 — Artificial Intelligence Standard

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

---

# Purpose

This standard defines how Artificial Intelligence is used within AITOS.

AI is an engineering accelerator, not an engineering authority.

All AI-generated work must remain reviewable, explainable, and traceable.

---

# Scope

This standard applies to:

- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Machine Learning models
- AI Coding Assistants
- AI Research Assistants
- AI Documentation Systems
- Autonomous Agents
- Future AI Systems

---

# AI Usage Principles

## Human Accountability

Humans remain responsible for all engineering, research, architectural, and financial decisions.

AI provides recommendations only.

---

## AI-Agnostic Design

No implementation should permanently depend on:

- OpenAI
- Anthropic
- Google
- xAI
- DeepSeek
- Any future vendor

AI providers must be replaceable.

---

## Transparency

Whenever practical:

- AI-generated content should be identifiable.
- AI-assisted modifications should remain reviewable.
- AI decisions should include supporting reasoning.

---

## Evidence-Based AI

AI outputs are hypotheses until validated.

Production decisions require evidence.

---

## Approved AI Tasks

AI may assist with:

- Documentation
- Code generation
- Refactoring
- Unit tests
- Architecture brainstorming
- Research summarization
- Risk analysis
- Knowledge extraction

---

## Restricted Tasks

AI must not independently:

- Approve production deployments
- Execute irreversible financial actions
- Modify governance documents
- Override security policies
- Ignore human review

---

## AI Validation

AI-generated work should be evaluated for:

- Correctness
- Security
- Maintainability
- Performance
- Explainability
- Compliance

---

## Prompt Management

Prompts should be:

- Version controlled
- Reusable
- Documented
- Continuously improved

---

This standard complements:
