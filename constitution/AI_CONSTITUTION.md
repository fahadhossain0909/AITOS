# AI Constitution

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the constitutional principles governing the use of Artificial Intelligence within the AITOS project.

AI is an engineering assistant—not an autonomous authority.

---

# Core Principle

AI augments human intelligence.

AI does not replace human accountability.

All critical engineering, research, financial, and governance decisions remain under human responsibility.

---

# Guiding Principles

## 1. AI Agnostic

AITOS must never depend permanently on a single AI provider.

Models should be replaceable without redesigning the system.

---

## 2. Human Oversight

Every AI-generated output must be reviewable.

High-impact decisions require human approval.

---

## 3. Transparency

AI-assisted outputs should be distinguishable from human-authored work whenever practical.

---

## 4. Explainability

Important AI recommendations should include reasoning or supporting evidence whenever possible.

---

## 5. Privacy

Sensitive information must never be unnecessarily exposed to AI services.

Confidential data should follow project security policies.

---

## 6. Continuous Evaluation

AI tools should be evaluated periodically for:

- Accuracy
- Reliability
- Cost
- Security
- Maintainability

---

# Approved AI Roles

AI may assist with:

- Documentation
- Architecture
- Code Generation
- Testing
- Code Review
- Research
- Brainstorming
- Refactoring

AI must not make irreversible production decisions independently.

---

# Constitutional Rule

Human responsibility is never delegated to artificial intelligence.
