# Design Philosophy

---

# Philosophy Statement

AITOS is designed around the belief that software quality depends more on engineering discipline than on technology choices.

Architecture should survive technology changes.

Knowledge should survive personnel changes.

Documentation should survive implementation changes.

---

# Core Design Principles

## Knowledge First

Knowledge is the primary asset.

Documentation, evidence, and research are treated as first-class citizens.

---

## Modularity

Every subsystem should be independently replaceable.

Dependencies should be minimized.

Interfaces should remain stable.

---

## AI Independence

No component should depend permanently on a specific AI provider.

The platform should support replacing AI models without redesigning the architecture.

---

## Documentation Driven Engineering

Documentation precedes implementation.

Every architectural decision should be documented.

---

## Evidence Driven Development

Research produces evidence.

Evidence supports decisions.

Decisions guide implementation.

---

## Security by Design

Security is integrated into architecture from the beginning.

It is never an afterthought.

---

## Simplicity

Prefer simple solutions over complex ones.

Complexity should be introduced only when justified by measurable benefits.

---

## Maintainability

Future contributors should understand the system with minimal effort.

Readable architecture is more valuable than clever implementation.

---

## Scalability

The system should support gradual expansion without major redesign.

---

## Human Governance

AI provides recommendations.

Humans remain responsible for final decisions.
